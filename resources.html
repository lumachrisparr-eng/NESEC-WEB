Our Library

We believe that great learners are built not just through instruction, but through exposure to a wide variety of high-quality materials.

Learning computer engineering is a journey that extends far beyond the classroom. The resources available on this page have been hand-picked to support every stage of that journey — from understanding foundational concepts to exploring cutting-edge research.

Everything you find here has been carefully evaluated for accuracy, depth, clarity, and relevance to computer engineering students. Whether you prefer structured reading, interactive tutorials, or hands-on experimentation, you will find resources suited to your learning style.

Resource Types

01 — Study Notes

PDF Study Notes

Our library of study notes provides concise, well-organized summaries of key concepts across all major course topics. Available in PDF format, these notes are ideal for exam revision, quick reference, or supplementing your course materials. Each set of notes is authored by subject matter experts and reviewed for accuracy. Topics include circuit analysis, algorithm design, operating systems, computer architecture, and much more.

02 — Tutorials

Step-by-Step Guides

Our tutorials are designed to walk you through both conceptual understanding and practical implementation. Each guide breaks down complex tasks into manageable steps, making it easy to follow along even if you are encountering the topic for the first time. From setting up a development environment to building your first neural network, our tutorials cover a vast spectrum of engineering tasks.

03 — Textbooks

Recommended Textbooks

Books remain one of the most reliable sources of in-depth knowledge. We have compiled a list of highly recommended textbooks that are widely used in universities and praised by engineering professionals worldwide. These books provide the depth and rigor that online content often lacks, and they are excellent companions to any course on this platform.

04 — Research Papers

Research Papers

For students who want to go beyond the curriculum and engage with the frontiers of knowledge, we provide access to curated research papers from leading journals and conferences. These papers cover emerging areas such as neuromorphic computing, quantum algorithms, edge AI, 5G network architecture, and more. Reading research papers is an essential skill for any engineer who wants to contribute to the advancement of the field.

05 — Open-Source Tools

Free Dev Tools

The best engineers are those who know how to leverage the right tools effectively. We have curated a list of open-source tools that are widely used in the computer engineering industry. These tools are free to use, actively maintained, and backed by strong community support.

06 — Practice Problems

Practice Problems

Mastery comes from practice. Our collection of practice problems spans all difficulty levels and covers every major topic taught on the platform. Each problem comes with hints, a model solution, and an explanation of the underlying concept. Whether you are preparing for an exam, a coding interview, or simply trying to reinforce what you have learned, our problem sets are an invaluable resource.

Open-Source Tools

The right tools, free to use.

The best engineers are those who know how to leverage the right tools effectively. We have curated a list of open-source tools that are widely used in the computer engineering industry.

Visual Studio Code — A powerful, lightweight code editor with extensive extensions for virtually every programming language and framework.

KiCad — A professional-grade PCB design tool used for creating electronic circuit schematics and board layouts.

Wireshark — A network protocol analyzer that lets you capture and inspect data packets traveling across a network.

QEMU — An open-source emulator for testing and running operating systems and embedded firmware without physical hardware.

TensorFlow — Google's open-source machine learning framework, widely used in both research and production AI applications.
